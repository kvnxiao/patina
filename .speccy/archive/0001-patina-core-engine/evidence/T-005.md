# Evidence — SPEC-0001 / T-005

Paper trail for per-machine state directory resolution
(`patina-core::state_dir`) covering REQ-016. T-005 owns layout
resolution and idempotent tree creation only; the lazily-created
files `profile` (T-007), `default_repo` (T-003), and `lock` (T-013)
belong to their owning subsystems. CHK-027 requires a full
`patina apply --yes` to verify that `.plan` / `.COMMIT` files and a
`backups/<ts>/` subdirectory are written — that end-to-end behaviour
is judgment-only here and is demonstrated by the journal task
(T-010 / T-012) and the executor task (T-014) once they land. The
parser-/layout-level prerequisite that the journal and backups
directories exist at the OS-correct location is demonstrated below.
